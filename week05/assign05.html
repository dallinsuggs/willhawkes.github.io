A Hungarian-American Story

Hello, my name is Dallin, and here is the story of how I and my wife Nelli met and formed a family. I grew up in Sacramento, CA in the United States, and Nelli grew up in Kölesd, Hungary.

Way back, a mere two and a half years ago, I was just approaching the final weeks of serving a mission in Hungary for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. One of the missionaries I knew mentioned a pretty girl to me that he and his companion were meeting with that day. I didn't think much of it. Meanwhile, he told this girl all about myself. After coming home from my mission, Nelli added me as a friend on Facebook and we started getting to know each other via video calls and messages. That summer she visited Utah, where I was then living, and we got to spend two weeks together.

Right at the end of Nelli's visit to the US, we submitted the paperwork for her to get a fiancée visa, with which she would then be able to move to Utah permanently and we could get married. Following submitting the paperwork, a painfully slow and agonizingly long 5 months trundled by before the application was approved, and after that another 2 months before she was called in for an interview. Fortunately we were able to alleviate the difficulty of each other's extended physical absence when I visited Hungary at the end of that year.

After another two weeks together (this time in Hungary) Nelli was finally able to go to her interview, and they granted her the visa! Only two months after my visit to Hungary she was back in the US again, this time with all of her belongings, here to stay. The next several days were a busy time, and on February 25th of 2020 we were married! We of course had an amazing time, and while Nelli's family members couldn't be present, she already befriended some of my extended family who were there to support us.

While in America Nelli discovered In 'n' Out, one of my personal favorite restaurants, and she fell in love with it much as I. So of course, only minutes after pictures were taken following the wedding, we had to stop by for a tasteful (tasty?) photo in our wedding getups at the locale venue. Fortunately a kind soul was available to act as cameraman, and now we can look back fondly at this moment.

Time passed, but not too much time, and we added someone to our family. Elanor Vivien Suggs was born at the end of the same year right in the middle of the COVID19 craze. Since then she has grown a lot, and so have we (her in size and mental capacity, we in patience and the like). We love each other and feel incredibly blessed that Nelli's immigration worked out (after a lot of blood sweat and tears, as they say) and we are able to be together.
